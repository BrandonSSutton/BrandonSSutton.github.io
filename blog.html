What is the truth about mental illnesses?


Making mental illnesses known to the world!

                  My write for change topic is centered around mental health awareness. I chose this topic for multiple reasons The most notable reason is that mental health issues are the leading cause of suicide in our country. I want to try to to alleviate that by educating the majority about mental illnesses. Another reason I chose this topic, is there is a shocking number of people living in America that believe mental health issues are all made up for an excuse not to do work. While yes it is true that some people do pretend to have a condition in order to get out of doing work, I hope to show these people that the majority of people who say they have a certain issue, actually have it. I will accomplish this through explaining the facts, symptoms, and common actions of those with various mental health issues. On the other side of that I will also be going into detail on how one might fake these symptoms and what you should look for in general to tell if someone really has a condition or not. I aim to educate everyone on how mental illness operate, and show how we can help those who experience these issues. I may even shed light on the subject for someone who thinks they may have an issue, making them realize that maybe that is not the case after all. And for those suffering through something, perhaps this will make them realize that they are not alone, and can go talk to someone. Often times the biggest issue is we don’t know who we can talk too. I will be answer that question in my research as well. This topic has affected my life in more ways than one. The first example I have to offer would be my friend. We will call him Jim. Jim has depression and although he is on medication for it, it does not always work. Sometimes I will receive messages or calls from Jim, saying how he feels like life is pointless, and nobody cares about him, and he doesn’t feel like he would be missed if he just ended it all right now. I do my best to assure him that I would miss him, as would many other people. However, this is difficult to explain to someone who has depression as they feel so low, and may even think that you are lieing to make them feel better. My next example goes back to when I was a young boy back in elementary school. I had a young friend we will call Bob. Bob had Asperger syndrome. He was a little awkward around people, and didn’t really understand right from wrong in the same way you or I would. Bob was a really kind person, and a great friend, he was just a little slower, and a little different. One had to be more patient with Bob as to not upset him, or to help him understand why something is the way it is. People who experience Asperger’s, they sometimes do not understand that there is something wrong with them, but there are also high functioning people with Asperger’s who are fully aware they have the syndrome, however, are unable to control it. My final example would be my uncle who sadly passed away when I was around 7. He was diagnosed with bipolar disorder. He had voices in his head telling him to do awful, awful things. He never thought he could talk about it with anyone, nor did he want to. He was raised in the belief that men do not discuss their issues, so he tried to deal with his issues. He fought for 36 years before ending it all. Leaving his family devastated to find out what he had been going through, knowing we had no clue. Thats how depression works a lot of the time. You would never know that someone in your life is depressed, because they may be smiling all the time, and laughing with you. They never talk about it, because they feel like a burden to you. This is why it is important to give people with mental conditions a way to talk about their issues, and be more aware in general of how these things affect these poor people. In the future I expect to run into more people with different mental disorders, I have yet to meet anyone with turret’s, or multiple personalities disorder, however if I do, I feel it would be nice to be educated about it more so I don’t accidentally address them in an improper manner.

                  I chose to go with the option of Duoethnography. I went with this option, because I wanted to give someone battling a mental health disorder a chance to speak out and give their view point on this. Since I know a few people that battle mental health, I figured this would be the easiest method to carry out, and considering all that has happened in the last two weeks, It was much easier to conduct an interview over a chat platform, than to attempt to observe someone out in public. Luckily, at least one person I know was comfortable discussing her ADHD, and did not mind my curiosity.

                  I spoke with a friend of mine, her name is Aubrey. The reason I decided to speak with her, is because I knew that she would be the least busy out of my friends, and I knew she was comfortable talking about these kinds of things. Questions I asked included: how do people act when they first meet you vs having known you for a while, How do your friends act vs your parents, how is everyday life like with ADHD. With these questions I got some really interesting answers. I conducted this interview online, since I had no other options at the time.

                  I cannot say I enjoyed the field work process. It felt to me like I was more doing this for research than for my own curiosity, and that made it feel like more work than it should have, but I cannot say I would have done this any differently. The interview went smoothly, and the hours time went by relatively quickly. It was not difficult to come up with questions I needed to ask, or to weed out what information was most important. It probably went smoother considering I was talking to a friend instead of a stranger.

                  My field work does help to inform my topic, because It fills in gaps between what the research I have found says, and what a someones personal experience with the disorder is like. For instance, the research says that people have difficulty focusing, but it is unclear how that really affects someone in every day life, Aubrey showed that its like unable to focus on what you want to focus on, so when she is trying to focus. This can affect one when attempting to find a job, or clean their house, or complete a task at an existing job. Which can lead to loss of said job, an unsanitary environment, or could even cause anxiety. My expectations were denied with ADHD, I expected it to be similar to a huge issue, but that is not always the case. Sometimes ADHD is a huge issue, and people are bouncing off the walls, but most of the time, that is not the case. And in Aubrey’s case, one can calm down over time.

                  I can use this information in my data bank to show how a mental disorder may affect individuals differently. There is always a common stigma that goes along with most mental disorders, and usually, there are key signs that people look for. As Aubrey showed me, those stigmas are rarely accurate, and the signs that most people look for aren’t displayed by everyone. While one person may display 3-4 symptoms of a mental illness, the person to the left may display all, and the person to the right may only display one. That doesn’t mean that one person is more likely to have the disorder over the other, it just means that there is more too it than just looking for key symptoms. Aubrey explained that she used to be super high energy, bouncing off the walls as one usually expected from someone from ADHD, however as she has gotten older, she has mellowed out a lot and because of this, I would not know she has ADHD just by looking at her, it would take a lot of time spent with her to even come close to realizing she does.
